Does it still happen today?

Although redlining is now illegal, neighborhoods that were redlined in the past face many more problems like pollution, fewer resources, and even worse health outcomes. Even though redlining no longer exists, we still see the impact it left on our communities today.

What can we do to Combat the Effects of Redling?

Steps recommended to reverse the effects of redlining include studying it's history and putting responsible, active people in power. By doing this we may prevent the past from repeating and we'll have people properly revising policies that will lead to the best outcome for the majority of a population than just a selected few.
